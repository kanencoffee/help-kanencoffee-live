DeLonghi Error 1512 — What It Means & How to Fix

General Alarm subtype on Magnifica S: causes, brew-unit reset, and when to service

  • What Is Error 1512?

    Error 1512 is a General Alarm subtype on DeLonghi Magnifica S machines, closely related to Error 1101. Where 1101 typically fires at startup or idle when the brew unit isn't in the home position, 1512 fires mid-cycle when the brew unit doesn't reach its expected target position in time. Think of it as 1101's "action" sibling.

    The fix path is similar: start with a brew-unit reseat and clean. About half of 1512 cases clear that way. The other half tend to be hardware — a worn position sensor, a dying brew motor, or a piston seal that's causing excess drag.

  • Affected DeLonghi Models

    • DeLonghi Magnifica S (primary — ECAM22/23)
    • DeLonghi Magnifica XS
    • DeLonghi Magnifica EVO (occasional)
    • Some PrimaDonna S / Class models
    • Dinamica / Dinamica Plus (rare)

  • Common Causes

    1. Brew unit piston drag — old seals or caked grounds add friction the motor can't overcome in time
    2. Brew unit position sensor drift — the microswitch reports "not home" when it actually is (or vice versa)
    3. Brew motor torque loss — motor still runs but can't finish the cycle fast enough
    4. Loose brew-unit harness connector — intermittent signal back to the board
    5. Control board timing drift — rare, typically on machines 8+ years old

  • DIY Reset Procedure (Try This First)

    1. Power the machine OFF at the rear hard switch
    2. Open the service door and remove the brew unit (press the two red release buttons)
    3. Rinse under warm water — work the black lever to move the piston back and forth until it slides freely
    4. If the piston is sticky or stiff, soak in a cup of fresh water with a cleaning tablet for 15 minutes, then rinse
    5. Inspect the piston seal (the round black o-ring at the top of the piston) for cracks or hardness
    6. Air-dry 5 minutes, reinsert until the firm click
    7. Run one hot-water flush, then attempt a single espresso
    8. If Error 1512 returns, repeat once more with extra attention to the piston lever moving smoothly

  • If That Doesn't Work

    • Brew unit piston seal is likely worn — add friction that keeps timing out
    • Brew motor torque is dropping — often makes a slightly weaker sound on each cycle
    • Position microswitch is dirty or worn and reports the wrong position
    • The brew-unit harness to the chassis connector has backed out

  • When to Call Professional Service

    • Error 1512 returns after 2 clean reseats
    • Brew motor sounds progressively weaker each cycle
    • Piston lever feels notably stiff even after cleaning
    • Error 1512 appears alongside 1101 or 1026
    • The machine completes the cycle physically but still throws 1512 at the end

DeLonghi Error 2257 — What It Means & How to Fix

No Water / Prime Pump fault on Magnifica S/XS (2257.1 & 2262 variants)

  • What Is Error 2257?

    Error 2257 (often shown as 2257.1, and very close to the 2262 variant) is DeLonghi's "no water / prime pump" fault. The machine's flow meter isn't registering water movement when the pump runs. Nine times out of ten it's a simple air lock: the tank ran dry, or the machine was moved, and air in the intake line stops the pump from priming.

    On rare occasions, 2257 points to a clogged intake filter, a stuck flow-meter impeller, or a pump that's losing output. The standard priming routine clears the majority of cases in under two minutes.

  • Affected DeLonghi Models

    • DeLonghi Magnifica S (ECAM22.110, 23.120, 23.460)
    • DeLonghi Magnifica XS
    • DeLonghi Magnifica EVO (ECAM29x — shows 2262 more commonly)
    • Dinamica / Dinamica Plus
    • Some PrimaDonna models

  • Common Causes

    1. Air lock after the tank emptied — the #1 cause; running the tank dry pulls air into the intake line
    2. Tank not fully reseated — the valve at the base of the tank doesn't open if the tank isn't pushed in firmly
    3. Intake filter clogged — limescale or water-filter debris has sealed the mesh at the base of the tank
    4. Flow meter stuck — impeller seized with scale, pump runs but board sees no flow
    5. Weakening vibration pump — classic at 6+ years of daily use; output drops below flow-meter threshold

  • DIY Reset Procedure (Try This First)

    1. Fill the water tank to the MAX line with fresh water
    2. Remove the tank, inspect the mesh at the base for scale/debris — rinse under the faucet if dirty
    3. Reseat the tank firmly — you should feel the valve click when it's fully home
    4. Place a large mug under the hot-water spout (not the coffee spout)
    5. Press hot water — let it run for 30 seconds or until flow is steady and vigorous
    6. Repeat steps 4–5 two more times to fully purge air
    7. Run 2–3 blank espresso cycles (brew without putting in a cup of beans) to purge the coffee circuit
    8. Power-cycle: off at the rear switch, wait 30 seconds, on

  • If That Doesn't Work

    • If water flow from the hot-water spout is weak or sputtering, the pump is likely failing
    • If the pump sounds strained or hums without producing flow, the impeller or piston is bad
    • If flow is fine from the spout but 2257 still triggers during espresso, the flow meter is stuck or failing
    • If the machine was recently descaled and the error appeared immediately, the descaler may have dislodged scale that's now blocking a hose — requires chassis inspection

  • When to Call Professional Service

    • Error 2257 returns after 3 complete priming cycles
    • Weak or sputtering water flow from the hot-water spout
    • Strained pump buzz with no water output
    • Pool of water under the machine (pump seal leak)
    • Error appeared immediately after a descale

Connect Your Analytics

Enter your GA4 Property ID and OAuth Client ID, then sign in with Google to load your dashboard.

Your data never leaves your browser — this dashboard connects directly to Google Analytics using your login credentials. No data is sent to any third-party server.
1
Find your GA4 Property ID
Go to analytics.google.com → click the gear icon (Admin, bottom-left) → in the Property column, click Property Settings → copy the Property ID (a number like 123456789). Enter just the number, no "properties/" prefix.
2
Create an OAuth Client ID
Go to console.cloud.google.com → create a project called Kanen Analytics → enable the Google Analytics Data API → go to Credentials → click Create Credentials → OAuth 2.0 Client ID → choose Web application → under Authorized JavaScript origins add https://help.kanencoffee.com → click Create → copy the Client ID.
3
Configure OAuth Consent Screen
In Google Cloud Console → OAuth consent screen → set User Type to External → add your Google account as a Test user → save. This is required before the sign-in button will work.
4
Sign in
Enter both values above and click Sign in with Google. A popup will appear asking you to authorize read-only access to Google Analytics. Once approved, your dashboard loads automatically.

Why it matters
Traffic overview is your core health metric. It gives you a baseline to compare week-over-week trends and measure the impact of any marketing changes, SEO updates, or promotions. For a local repair shop, consistent traffic growth means your visibility in Berkeley and the broader Bay Area is improving.
How to use it
Check weekly. A sudden drop usually signals a technical issue (broken links, indexing problem) or a Google algorithm update. Steady week-over-week growth means healthy SEO. Compare the 7-day and 30-day views after any marketing activity — blog posts, Google Business Profile updates, or social posts — to see their direct effect on traffic.

    4. Goal Events & Conversions
    Top events by count — key conversion signals
    Why it matters
    Events are your most direct revenue signal. Phone number clicks and contact form submissions translate directly to repair bookings. "Book Appointment" button clicks show intent. Tracking these lets you see whether your website is converting visitors into customers — not just generating traffic.
    How to use it
    Check weekly. Set a conversion target (e.g. 20 phone clicks/week) and track against it. If phone_click drops, check whether the phone number is prominently displayed on mobile — that's where most local searches happen. If scroll depth is low on service pages, the content may be too long or not compelling enough above the fold.

    GA4's Engagement Rate counts sessions where a visitor stayed >10 seconds, viewed more than one page, or triggered a conversion event.
